Medical Background

Understanding diabetic retinopathy and its clinical significance

What is Diabetic Retinopathy?

Diabetic retinopathy is a diabetes complication that affects the eyes. It's caused by damage to the blood vessels of the light-sensitive tissue at the back of the eye (retina).

Why Early Detection Matters

Early detection and treatment can reduce the risk of blindness by 95%. Regular eye examinations are crucial for people with diabetes, as diabetic retinopathy often has no early warning signs.

Severity Levels

  • No DR: No signs of diabetic retinopathy
  • Mild: Microaneurysms only
  • Moderate: More than just microaneurysms but less than severe
  • Severe: Extensive intraretinal hemorrhages and microaneurysms
  • Proliferative DR: New blood vessels growing on the retina
